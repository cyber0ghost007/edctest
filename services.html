Mathematics:

Study numbers, shapes and patterns. Use logic and reasoning to solve problems. EDC offers mathematics from early childhood to high school and pre-university preparatory “bridging basics” courses. This subject is also best for students and professionals from non-mathematical background to develop their basic mastery in maths.

General Science:

HRM:

Learn the art to devise formal systems for the management of organisations and help maximize employee services as per employer goals. This subject is ideal for students and professionals to develop strategic techniques in managing people, work place environment and a positive work culture.

Management and Finance:

Learn to effectively manage business funds to meet the goals ot any organisation. This subject is best for students and professionals aiming to develop a better understanding on money management especially in a cooperate context.

Small Business Management:

Develop the skills to envision, develop and manage all aspects of a small independently owned company. Small businesses are very prominent and therefore this course is ideal for students and professionals aiming to affiliate or are already part of a small business.

International Business:

The growth of companies cross borders are ongoing and insights into the global business and economic climates is equally important for proper managing of such businesses. This course aims to teach the internationalisation of enterprises, ideal for students and professionals aiming to eventually step foot in the international market.

Economics:

Resources and facilities are important and must be utilised wisely. This subject enlightens learners to understand production, distribution and consumption of goods and services, ideal for students and professionals to develop wanting to upskill in proper resource management strategies.

Business Communication:

The art of conveyance is essential and in the business world, a key factor to the success of the business. The eloquence, articulation, clarity and persuasion a business communicator uses strongly impacts the morale image and efficiency of the business. This subject is ideal for students and professionals who wish to have more effective command in business communication, a multidisciplinary art that can serve in all arenas of life.

Business Law:

Rules and regulations are to ensure ethical and fair practices for everyone. This course deals with set of guidelines specific for the business setting and is best for students and professionals aiming to affiliate with any business or cooperate organisation in the future.

International Trade:

An old states that, success is never built on an island. When trade takes place between many nations, a variety of goods can be accessible. This subject teaches the underlying principle of such capitals, goods and services across international borders, ideal for students and professionals wishing to upskill in the international trade concepts.

Computer & IT Courses:

Living in the information age, technology has become just as important as the ability to read and write. This course teaches multiple aspects of the use of gadgets to efficiently draw out planned tasks and communicate via tech tools. This course is dynamic with various topic options of choice to explore, ideal for students and professionals aiming to gain more confidence and ability in virtual technologies.

Hospitality:

Multibillion dollar industry, which at its basic stage, is a relationship between a guest and a host. UMAT:

Stands for Undergraduate Medicine and Health Sciences Admission Test is used specifically to assist with the selection of students into the medicine, dentistry and health science degree programs.

GAMSAT:

The Graduate Australian Medical School Admissions Test is a test used to select candidates applying to study medicine, dentistry, optometry, physiotherapy, podiatry, pharmacy and veterinary science at Australian, British, and Irish universities for admission to their Graduate Entry Programmes.

PTE:

The Pearson Test of English Academic (PTE Academic) is the English test trusted by universities, collages and governments around the world.

IELTS:

The International English Language Testing System, or IELTS, is an international standardized test of English language proficiency for non-native English language speakers.

SAT:

The SAT is a standardized test widely used for college admissions in the United States. These tests measure the same skills and knowledge in ways that make sense for different grade levels, so it's easier for students, parents, and educators to monitor student progress.

MCAT:

The Medical College Admission Test (MCAT) is a computer-based standardized examination for prospective medical students in the United States, Australia, Canada, and Caribbean Islands. It is designed to assess problem-solving, critical thinking, written analysis and knowledge of scientific concepts and principles.

GRE:

The GRE (Graduate Record Examinations) is a standardized test taken by students who are applying to postgraduate courses in the US and in some other parts of the world.

MAT:

The Miller Analogies Test (MAT) is a test of verbal skills (vocabulary and comprehension) and analytical thinking which the test makers have designed to be a predictor of success in graduate school and professional life.
